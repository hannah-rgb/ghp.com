HANNAH
PARK
Grace Hannah Park navigates the spaces between type, motion, print, and code to question how medium shapes message. Her work often begins with noticing how a moving image can breathe new life into a static poster, or how certain ideas only make sense once printed, folded, or bound. Lately, she's been drawn to the tactile intelligence of street posters: their materiality, their presence and how these qualities might be translated into digital formats. With a practice grounded in both formal design and experimental tools, she treats graphic design as a responsive system rather than a fixed outcome.

CLIENT
PHOTO
Carnault is a luxury E-cigarette Brand that values Swiss precision and craftmanship.

As a graphic designer, my responsibility was to deliver a full brand guidebook for the brand. Considering the usage of logo, color, printed and digital materials that are produced inhouse, I developed a brand guideline.

The complete Brand book contains target group analysis, USPs, offerings, design principles, brand essence including brand mission, vision statement and value proposition. It was important to unify the tone of voice including slogans, sublimes and terminologies also for the continuity of the brand. Most importantly, the brand's main color green was always shifting depending on print situation and it was urgent to unify it to a curated scheme of colors including main green color, point gold color and other sub colors. Other than that it was a big scale of guideline setting work that included, moodboard, photography rules, logo rules, typography rules and even forms, slides and visual merchandise design.
